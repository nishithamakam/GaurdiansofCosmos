Radiation

This radiation can impact the telescope's sensitive instruments and detectors.

To mitigate this, the JWST is equipped with radiation shielding and its instruments are designed to operate in the presence of radiation.

Outgassing

These gases can contaminate sensitive optical surfaces.

Special materials and coatings are used on the JWST to minimize outgassing.

Extreme Temperature Variations

The JWST is designed to operate at extremely cold temperatures to maximize its infrared observations.

Its sunshield provides shade and temperature control for the instruments, keeping them colder than -370 degrees Fahrenheit (-223 degrees Celsius) to prevent interference from their own heat.

Solar Wind and Magnetic Fields

The solar wind, a stream of charged particles from the Sun, and magnetic fields in space can affect the telescope's operation, particularly its ability to maintain a stable orientation for observations. The spacecraft's attitude control system counteracts these influences.
